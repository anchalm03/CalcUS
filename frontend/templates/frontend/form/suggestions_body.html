GFN2-xTB Details
GFN2-xTB in a nutshell
×
Very fast, general method. Not available for some property calculations.

Good for:
  • Geometries (equilibrium structures, transition states and constrained structures)
  • Frequency modes
  • UV-Vis spectra

Not optimal for:
  • Accurate electronic energies
  • Exotic or uncommon compounds (may yield strange results)

  • Reference of UV-Vis calculations with sTDA-xTB: J. Chem. Phys., 2016, 145, 054103.
GFN-FF Details
GFN2-FF in a nutshell
×
Excessively fast method, even faster than GFN2-xTB. Does not handle bond formation or dissociation. Performs well for organic molecules containing main-group elements.

Good for:
  • Conformer generation (due to its speed)
  • Geometry and frequency modes, although GFN2-xTB should be preferred

Not optimal for:
  • Accurate electronic energies
  • Exotic or uncommon compounds (may yield strange results)
References
  • xtb software home
  • General reference: WIREs Comput. Mol. Sci., 2020, 11, e01493.
  • GFN-FF reference: Angew. Chem. Int. Ed., 2020, 59, 15665–15673.
  • Reference of implicit solvation models: J. Chem. Theory Comput., 2021, 17, 4250-4261.
M062X/Def2-SVP Details
M062X/Def2-SVP in a nutshell
×
Density functional theory (DFT) method constituted of the M062X functional and the Def2-SVP basis set. Multiple orders of magnitude slower than GFN2-xTB, but applicable to essentially any molecule.


Start with small calculations to see how long they take. Even with powerful compute nodes, these calculations can take multiple hours and even multiple days for large systems.

Good for:
  • Any calculation of small molecules
  • Obtaining accurate electronic energies

Not optimal for:
  • Large molecules (depending on the type of calculation and your computation time budget)
References
  • Reference of NWChem, the software running DFT calculations: J. Chem. Phys. 2020, 152, 184102.
  • Reference of the M062X functional: Theor. Chem. Acc. 2008, 120, 215–241.
  • Reference of the Def2 basis sets: Phys. Chem. Chem. Phys. 2005, 7, 3297-3305.
  • Reference of the effective core potentials for Def2 basis sets: Phys. Chem. Chem. Phys. 2006, 8, 1057-1065.
M062X/Def2-TZVP Details
M062X/Def2-TZVP in a nutshell
×
Density functional theory (DFT) method constituted of the M062X functional and the Def2-TZVP basis set. Provides publication-quality results, but requires significant computational power.

Start with very tiny calculations to see how long they take. Even with powerful compute nodes, these calculations can take many hours and even multiple days for common systems.

Good for:
  • Any calculation of tiny molecules
  • Obtaining very accurate electronic energies
  • Obtaining state-of-the-art results for scientific publications

Not optimal for:
  • Medium-sized molecules and larger (depending on the type of calculation and your computation time budget)
